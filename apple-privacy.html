Clickette Privacy Policy

At Clickette, we take the privacy of our users seriously. We do not collect any personal information from our users, and we do not track their online activity.

To provide relevant search results, we proxy search queries to Google. This means that we do not have access to any personal information related to the searches performed on our site. We do not collect or share any identifiable information, such as IP addresses, with third parties.

We use cookies on our website to differentiate between human users and bots. These cookies are necessary for the proper functioning of our site, and they do not collect any personal information. We may also use cookies to save certain preferences and settings on our website, such as the closing of compatibility alerts. This feature allows users to close an alert that is displayed if the website is not compatible with their browser or device. These cookies are first-party cookies, and we do not use any third-party cookies for tracking or advertising purposes. By using Clickette, you consent to the use of these cookies.

Managing Cookies

You can manage your cookies settings and opt out of cookie usage by following the instructions below:

  1. To block or delete cookies through your browser settings:
    • In Google Chrome: go to Settings > Privacy and security > Site Settings > Cookies and site data, and adjust your settings as desired.
    • In Mozilla Firefox: go to Options > Privacy and Security, and adjust the settings under the "Cookies and Site Data" section.
    • In Microsoft Edge: go to Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies, and adjust your settings as desired.
    • In Brave: go to Settings > Cookies and other site data, and adjust your settings as desired.

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

We are committed to complying with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and ensuring the privacy and security of our users' personal data. We have implemented appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure or access. These measures include the use of encryption technologies such as AES-256 and regular security audits to prevent unauthorized access, and the establishment of procedures for responding to data breaches and other security incidents.
